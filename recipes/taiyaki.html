taiyaki

Description

Taiyaki(pronounced "Tie-Yah-Kee") is the quintessential Japanese street food and a very popular dessert at festivals. Westerners often wonder why taiyaki, a dessert, is shaped like a fish. It originated from imagawayaki, a very similar pastry which is a round shape rather than a fish shape. The exact origins are unknown, but sometime during the Edo period in what is now Tokyo, marketing-savvy shops started shaping imagawayaki in the shape of a taiâ€”a type of fish known as Japanese sea bream. The unique design sold better and the wildly popular taiyaki as we know it came to be.

Ingredients

  • 170g of butter(12 tablespoons)
  • 65 grams of light brown sugar(8 tablespoons)
  • 100 grams egg(2 eggs)
  • 240 grams cake flour(1 1/2 cup)
  • 6 grams baking powder(2 teaspoons)
  • 155 grams warm milk(2/3 cup)
  • 10 grams honey(2 teaspoons)
  • 6 grams light soy sauce(2 teaspoons)
  • 4 grams vanilla extract(2 teaspoons)

Steps

  1. Mix together the milk,honey,soy sauce, and vanilla extract and set aside
  2. Cream the butter together with the sugar
  3. Beat in one egg, then the other
  4. Beat in half the flour, then the milk, then the other half of flour
